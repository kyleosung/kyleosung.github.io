No tutorials yet! Please fill out the Entry Form and prepare for tutorials to start in Week 2. Thank you!

Week 2

We aim to recall some tricky topics from precalculus such as trig identities, functions without an elementary inverse, and solving equations with exponential and logarithmic functions.

  • Week 2 T02 (PDF)
  • Week 2 T03 (PDF)

Week 3

This week we learn all about approaches: limits, continuity, and the Intermediate Value Theorem. We also define the derivative and use it to find equations of tangent lines.

  • Week 3 Blank (PDF)
  • Week 3 T02 (PDF)
  • Week 3 T03 (PDF)

Week 4

Differentiation requires following a number of rules (Power, Product, Quotient, and Chain). Let's figure out how to use them and apply them to Newton's Method and Implicit Functions.

  • Week 4 Blank (PDF)
  • Week 4 T02 (PDF)
  • Week 4 T03 (PDF)

Week 5

We continue to look at derivatives and their applications, focusing on inverse functions, hyperbolic functions, logarithmic differentiation, and the Extreme Value Theorem.

  • Week 5 Blank (PDF)
  • Week 5 T02 (PDF)
  • Week 5 T03 (PDF)

Week 6

Picking up the pace to prep for our first test, we investigate some tricky problems that we might not have come across yet, including higher order derivatives, domains of nested logarithm functions. We also review our right angle trigonometry.

  • Week 6 Blank (PDF)
  • Week 6 T02 (PDF)
  • Week 6 T03 (PDF)

Week 7

We return to applications of the derivative with optimization, L'Hopital's Rule, asymptotes, and another major theorem in Calculus: the Mean Value Theorem.

Week 8

Let's find out what happens when we add up a large amount of terms! Gauss's Trick, telescoping sums, and special series help us find these values, and let us start to approximate the area under a function by building a series of rectangles we call Riemann Sums. Can we find a pattern that might help us evaluate these areas more quickly?

Week 9

After learning about the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, we learn about how antidifferentiation can make our lives easier (and all of the challenges that come with it!).

Week 10

Continuing to investigate antidifferentiation, we develop some more techniques that help us evaluate integrals. We discover that in addition to our techniques to reverse power and chain rules, there is a way to reverse the product rule.

Week 11

With our newfound knowledge of integrals, we consider applications like finding volumes and the average value of a function. We find that there is a new way to express the Mean Value Theorem.

Week 12

Trigonometry comes back with substitutions that will help simplify integrals we might not have been able to solve otherwise. We also look at integration of rational functions using partial fractions, and integrate over arcs to wrap up the material for the course.
